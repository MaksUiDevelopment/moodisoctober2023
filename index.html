Who we are?
We are a group of ninth grade students from the Yoan Jabotinski School, Ahadshanim (literally translated: innovators). We made this site as part of the MUDIS (multidisciplinary training) project. The original theme of MUDIS was “Leadership and Activism.” We studied various Israeli and other leaders and how they dealt with various difficult situations. At this time, war began in Israel. And all of us, like the rest of the Israelis, had to become leaders ourselves to help our country. From the first day of the war, an operational headquarters (hamal) was opened in our school to collect necessary things and food for the army. Many former students of our school are now serving in the army, they pass on information about what their units need, and our teachers, students and parents collect and transfer what is needed to the army. But we thought that this is not the only way to help the country. We decided that people in other countries should also know about what is happening in our country, about Israel’s fight against terrorism. That's why we made this site.
We want people living in other countries to know about what is happening in Israel now. Along with the site, we have created a bot that receives photos, videos and information about the war, about what is happening on the home front and in the lives of ordinary people, about the heroism of the Israelis. Our bot can filter information; it does not accept materials containing physical violence or other horrors. We believe that people should receive information that is truthful but not scary.

“Shiral celebrates There are moments in life when a person buys his world. In one act, in one move. For Shiral Hogg, whose sister was murdered in the shocking attack in Kfar Gaza, it was a televised moment. Hogg was broadcast by Amir Ivegi on Channel 14 (and before that on Channel 12, and later on Channel 13), and beyond the personal testimony of a man who lost his world - he also brought with him another message. A message that sat in the hearts of quite a few Israelis for the channel where the interview was broadcast. "We are the people of Israel," he said, "right, left, lovers of the country. Ready to die for the country. If one more time one of you calls someone from the left or the right a 'traitor', we will not forgive you." This moment when the poison of the factional discourse on channel 14 was hit on the head live - was a huge moment of catharsis for many people that was kept firmly in their stomachs.”

“Danny Kushmaro Desperate situations, as we know, also lead to desperate measures. Danny Kushmaro, for many, many years, was a "classic" news anchor - a very clear middle figure, all-Israeli, that everyone could love. The protest surrounding the coup threw him a little off balance - and made him express his opinion. In the war, it increased: his excited texts at the beginning of every news release since the war, in which he insisted on bringing the truth as it is to the screen, are great moments of television but also of a human being who understands his role. Contrary to the Israeli instinct, which was born with the phrase "shoot quietly", war is exactly the time to illuminate and flood problems. This is the only chance that something will be fixed, in the most fateful campaign that Israel has ever known”
