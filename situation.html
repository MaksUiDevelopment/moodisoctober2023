What are we dealing with?
We face terrorist organizations like Hamas, Hezbollah and Islamic Jihad, all of which pose a regional and international threat. We further leverage our capabilities to provide humanitarian aid to countries worldwide in times of crisis and develop groundbreaking technologies and tactics in the field of defense. We focus on maintaining the security of Israeli civilians every single day but our activities aim to contribute to the overall safety of civilians everywhere.

Hamas is a Sunni Islamist Palestinian organization. It is defined as a terrorist organization by the State of Israel, as well as by many countries around the world, including the United States and the European Union. Its stated goal is the establishment of a Palestinian state on the entire territory of Israel. Since 2007, Hamas has controlled the Gaza Strip.
Every ground, aerial and naval operational activity and exercise we conduct is first and foremost carried out for the sake of defending the State of Israel and its civilians.
7 October
On October 7th, 2023, thousands of armed Hamas terrorists tore down large parts of the Gaza security fence using tractors, RPGs and explosives and invaded southern Israel. Simultaneously, Hamas terrorists in Gaza fired thousands of rockets toward Israel.

More than 50 days have passed since the massacre, and still more and more testimonies continue to flow from Hamas terrorists arrested by the security forces. Today (Monday) it was allowed to publish information that the terrorists Adham Hussa and Ismail Hussa passed on during their interrogation by the Shin Bet. The investigation shows that the two were observed on the kibbutz's security cameras, kidnapped foreign citizens, came with them to Shifa Hospital - and were recorded on the hospital's cameras.

The two terrorists infiltrated Israel on the morning of Saturday, October 7, with the aim of harming as many residents as possible - Israelis and non-Israelis. The two abducted several Thai workers and a Nepali citizen from the agricultural lands of Kibbutz Alumim, put them on an ambulance at the border - and drove with them to Shifa Hospital in the Gaza Strip.

What IDF (Israel defends forces) did in Gaza
Israel's war crimes
Following the IDF's retaliatory strike in the Gaza Strip, numerous critics began labeling it as a "war crime." Let's first understand what constitutes war crimes in legal terms and what is permitted by international law. We do not deny the suffering of the civilian population in the Gaza Strip. Instead, we inquire whether it aligns with the legal definition of a war crime. A war crime is a violation of international laws of war, primarily outlined in the Geneva Conventions. Examples include deliberate attacks on civilians, the use of human shields, taking civilians as hostages, and the torture of prisoners. The essence of the laws of war is to distinguish between civilians and enemies. Currently, there is an impression that HAMAS has used the Geneva Conventions as a guide for its actions. However, our inquiry is not whether HAMAS commits war crimes; rather, we are attempting to ascertain if Israel is doing so.
